🦖 Dino Bytes

Chomp into some bite-sized fun facts from every dino era!

🌄 Triassic Tidbits

Coelophysis may have traveled in packs—like prehistoric wolves. Herrerasaurus was one of the first true theropods. The Triassic landscape was mostly desert and drought-prone.

🌋 Jurassic Jumpscares

Stegosaurus had a brain the size of a walnut—no offense. Brachiosaurus could stretch up to 12 meters tall. Allosaurus fossils suggest they may have hunted in groups.

🌎 Cretaceous Crunch

Velociraptors were feathered and only about the size of a turkey. Parasaurolophus may have used its crest to trumpet across forests. T. rex's bite force was stronger than any animal alive today.

🧬 Bonus Bytes

Some dinosaurs had hollow bones like birds, helping them stay light on their feet. Fossilized dino poop—called coprolite—tells scientists what dinos ate. And the word “dinosaur” wasn’t coined until 1842 by Richard Owen, decades after fossils were first discovered!
